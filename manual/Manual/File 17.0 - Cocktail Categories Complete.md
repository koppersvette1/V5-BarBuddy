II. THE 7 MAJOR FAMILIES (Expanded)
1. THE ANCESTRALS (Old Fashioned Style)
Definition: The original definition of a cocktail (Spirit + Sugar + Water + Bitters).
Characteristics: No juice, no vermouth. Just sweetened spirit.
Technique: Always Stirred.
Sub-Types:
Improved Whiskey Cocktail: Adds a dash of Maraschino or Absinthe.
Sazerac: Adds Anise rinse.
2. THE SPIRIT-FORWARD (Martini/Manhattan Style)
Definition: Spirit modified by a lower-ABV wine (Vermouth, Sherry).
Characteristics: Silky, aromatic, high ABV.
Technique: Always Stirred.
The Negroni Branch: Adds a bitter liqueur (Campari) to the formula (1:1:1).
3. THE SOURS (Citrus Style)
Definition: The largest family. Spirit + Acid (Lemon/Lime) + Sugar.
Characteristics: Tart, refreshing, often foamy.
Technique: Always Shaken.
The Daisy Branch: Replaces simple syrup with a Liqueur (Margarita uses Cointreau, Sidecar uses Triple Sec).
4. THE HIGHBALLS (Carbonated)
Definition: Spirit lengthened with a non-alcoholic mixer.
Characteristics: Tall, lots of ice, refreshing, lower ABV.
Technique: Built in the glass.
The Mule Branch: Spirit + Lime + Ginger Beer.
The Collins Branch: A Sour + Soda Water.
5. THE TROPICAL / TIKI
Definition: Rum-based sours with complex sweeteners (Almond, Cinnamon) and multiple fruits.
Characteristics: Layered flavors, often high ABV ("Zombie"), crushed ice.
Technique: Shaken or Flash Blended.
6. THE SPARKLING
Definition: Cocktails topped with sparkling wine.
Characteristics: Celebratory, dry, effervescent.
Technique: Shake the base, strain, top with bubbles.
Examples: French 75 (Gin), Air Mail (Rum), Old Cuban (Mint).
7. THE DESSERT / FLIP
Definition: Drinks using Cream, Egg Yolk, or Sweet Liqueurs.
Characteristics: Thick texture, sweet, after-dinner.
Technique: Hard Shake (to froth).
Examples: Espresso Martini, Brandy Alexander, White Russian.
III. HOW TO IDENTIFY A CATEGORY (The "Blind Taste" Test)
If you are served a mystery drink, ask:
Is it clear or cloudy?
Clear: It's Stirred (Old Fashioned/Martini family).
Cloudy: It has juice/shake (Sour/Tiki family).
Is it bubbly?
Yes: Highball or Sparkling family.
Is it bitter?
Yes: Negroni/Aperitivo family.
Is it creamy?
Yes: Dessert family.
