II. FLAVOR CORRECTION SCIENCE
1. The Salt Hack (Saline Solution)
The Science: Sodium ions bind to bitter receptors on your tongue, temporarily blocking them. This allows sweetness and citrus to shine through.
The Recipe: Mix 20g Sea Salt into 80g Water (20% solution).
Usage: Add 3-5 drops to any drink that tastes too bitter or "muddy." It is the MSG of cocktails.
2. The Acid Adjust
Lemon vs. Lime: Lemon is softer/sweeter. Lime is sharper/more aggressive.
The Fix: If a drink is "cloying" (sticky sweet), adding more spirit won't help. You need acid to cut the sugar. Add 0.25 oz fresh lemon juice.
3. The Texture Fix
Thin/Watery Mouthfeel: Usually means you shook with "wet" ice or didn't use a rich enough syrup.
The Fix: Add 0.25 oz Rich Demerara Syrup (2:1 sugar). The extra sugar viscosity adds weight.

IV. EQUIPMENT HACKS (The "MacGyver" List)
No Shaker? Use a Mason jar with a tight lid.
No Jigger?
1 Shot Glass = 1.5 oz
1 Tablespoon = 0.5 oz
1 Teaspoon = 0.16 oz (approx)
No Muddler? Use the handle of a wooden spoon.
No Mixing Glass? Use the pint glass part of your shaker or a heavy measuring cup.
No Fine Strainer? Use a clean tea strainer or hold the shaker gate very nearly closed.
V. THE "LAST RESORT" (Save the Party)
If a batched drink or pitcher is truly ruined:
Turn it into a Spritz: Dilute the mistake with lots of Club Soda or Prosecco. Bubbles hide many sins.
Make it a Slushie: Blend it with lots of ice. The cold numbs the tongue to flavor imbalances.
Add Bitters: A heavy dose of Angostura can shift a drink from "bad fruity" to "complex spiced."
