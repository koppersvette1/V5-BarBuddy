RECIPE #39: PENICILLIN
Category: Sour (Shaken)
Base Spirit: Scotch (Blended + Single Malt)
ABV Level: Medium (20%+)
Complexity: Medium
Glass: Rocks
Ice: Large cube
Method: Shake
Time to Make: 3 minutes
Standard Spec - "The Cure":
2 oz Blended Scotch (Famous Grouse or Monkey Shoulder)
0.75 oz Fresh Lemon Juice
0.75 oz Honey-Ginger Syrup
0.25 oz Peated Single Malt Scotch (Laphroaig 10 or Ardbeg) - FLOAT ON TOP
Candied Ginger garnish
Step-by-Step Instructions:
Combine blended scotch, lemon, and syrup in shaker
Shake with ice
Strain over fresh large ice cube
The Float: Gently pour the peated scotch over the back of a spoon so it sits on top
Garnish with candied ginger
Smoke Pairing (Optional):
Best Wood: Pear
Time: 10-12 seconds
Note: Pear wood enhances the ginger notes without fighting the peat.
Taste Profile:
Sweet: 3/5 | Sour: 3/5 | Bitter: 0/5 | Boozy: 3/5 | Spicy/Smoky: 5/5
Common Mistakes:
Mistake
Why it fails
The Fix
Shaking the Single Malt
Mixes the smoke in, losing the aroma
You MUST float the smoky scotch on top
No Ginger
Just a honey sour
Fresh ginger syrup is essential for the "bite"

Why It Works:
It mimics the curative properties of a hot toddy but served cold.
The float ensures the first smell is pure smoke, but the taste is sweet and spicy.
History & Context:
Created by Sam Ross at Milk & Honey in NYC in 2005. It is widely considered the most successful "Modern Classic" cocktail, having spread to almost every bar in the world in less than 20 years.

RECIPE #40: ESPRESSO MARTINI
Category: Dessert (Shaken)
Base Spirit: Vodka
ABV Level: Medium (15%+)
Complexity: Easy
Glass: Martini or Coupe
Ice: None
Method: Shake
Time to Make: 3 minutes
Standard Spec - "The Wake Up Call":
2 oz Vodka
1 oz Fresh Espresso (Hot is fine)
0.5 oz Coffee Liqueur (Kahlúa or Mr. Black)
0.25 oz Simple Syrup (Adjust based on sweetness of liqueur)
3 Coffee Beans (Garnish)
Step-by-Step Instructions:
Brew espresso
Add all ingredients to shaker with LOTS of ice
Shake HARD: You need to aerate the coffee oils to get the foam
Strain quickly into chilled glass
Place 3 beans in a petal pattern on the foam
Smoke Pairing (Optional):
Best Wood: Maple or Pecan
Time: 8-10 seconds
Note: Nutty smoke pairs perfectly with coffee.
Taste Profile:
Sweet: 3/5 | Sour: 0/5 | Bitter: 2/5 | Boozy: 3/5 | Coffee: 5/5
Common Mistakes:
Mistake
Why it fails
The Fix
Weak Shake
No foam layer
Shake until your arm hurts
Old Coffee
Tastes stale/sour
Fresh espresso creates the best crema foam

Why It Works:
The vodka gets out of the way, letting the coffee and sugar do the work.
The foam provides a creamy texture without any dairy.
History & Context:
London, 1983. A famous model walked into the Soho Brasserie and asked Dick Bradsell for a drink that would "Wake me up and f*** me up." He mixed vodka with espresso, and the legend was born.

RECIPE #41: WHITE RUSSIAN
Category: Dessert (Built)
Base Spirit: Vodka
ABV Level: Low-Medium (15%+)
Complexity: Easy
Glass: Rocks
Ice: Cubes
Method: Build
Time to Make: 1 minute
Standard Spec - "The Dude":
2 oz Vodka
1 oz Coffee Liqueur (Kahlúa)
1 oz Heavy Cream (or Half & Half)
Step-by-Step Instructions:
Fill glass with ice
Add vodka and Kahlúa
Stir to combine
Float the cream on top gently
Serve layered (drinker stirs it in)
Smoke Pairing (Optional):
Best Wood: N/A
Note: Smoke makes the cream taste strange.
Taste Profile:
Sweet: 4/5 | Sour: 0/5 | Bitter: 1/5 | Boozy: 2/5 | Creamy: 5/5
Common Mistakes:
Mistake
Why it fails
The Fix
Using Milk
Too watery
You need the fat content of cream/half-and-half for texture

Why It Works:
It is alcoholic coffee ice cream.
History & Context:
Created in the late 1940s (the "Black Russian" came first). Adding cream made it "White." Immortalized by Jeff Bridges as "The Dude" in the movie The Big Lebowski.

RECIPE #42: SMOKED OLD FASHIONED
Category: Smoked Specialty
Base Spirit: Whiskey
ABV Level: High (30%+)
Complexity: Medium (Equipment needed)
Glass: Rocks
Method: Smoke & Stir
Time to Make: 4 minutes
Standard Spec - "Campfire Standard":
2 oz Bourbon
0.25 oz Maple Syrup (works better with smoke than sugar)
3 dashes Angostura Bitters
Orange Peel
Smoke: Cherry Wood
Step-by-Step Instructions:
Build the drink in the glass (Whiskey, syrup, bitters, ice)
Stir to chill
Place glass in smoking cloche or use top-down smoker
Fill with Cherry wood smoke
Let sit for 10-15 seconds (don't over-smoke!)
Reveal and garnish
Why It Works:
The most popular smoked cocktail. The smoke clings to the ice and glass, providing aroma with every sip.
Taste Profile:
Sweet: 3/5 | Sour: 0/5 | Bitter: 2/5 | Boozy: 5/5 | Smoky: 5/5
Common Mistakes:
Mistake
Why it fails
The Fix
Over-smoking
Tastes like an ashtray
10 seconds is plenty; aroma is the goal, not flavor saturation

RECIPE #43: SMOKED MANHATTAN
Category: Smoked Specialty
Base Spirit: Rye Whiskey
ABV Level: High (30%+)
Complexity: Medium
Glass: Coupe
Method: Smoke & Stir
Time to Make: 4 minutes
Standard Spec - "The Smoky City":
2 oz Rye Whiskey
1 oz Sweet Vermouth
2 dashes Bitters
Smoke: Oak Wood
Step-by-Step Instructions:
Smoke the EMPTY glass first (rinse method)
Trap smoke in glass with a coaster
Stir drink in mixing glass
Release smoke from glass and pour drink in immediately
Why It Works:
Smoking the glass imparts a lighter touch than smoking the liquid, preserving the delicate vermouth.
RECIPE #44: OAXACA OLD FASHIONED
Category: Smoked Specialty (Ingredient based)
Base Spirit: Tequila + Mezcal
ABV Level: High (30%+)
Complexity: Medium
Glass: Rocks
Method: Stir
Time to Make: 2 minutes
Standard Spec - "Liquid Smoke":
1.5 oz Reposado Tequila
0.5 oz Mezcal (This provides the smoke)
1 tsp Agave Nectar
2 dashes Mole Bitters
Flamed Orange Peel
Step-by-Step Instructions:
Combine ingredients in mixing glass
Stir with ice
Strain over large cube
Flame the peel: Squeeze peel through a lighter flame over the drink
Why It Works:
You don't need a smoke gun; the Mezcal provides natural smoke flavor.
History & Context:
Created by Phil Ward at Death & Co in NYC in 2007. This drink single-handedly kickstarted the Mezcal craze in the US.

RECIPE #49: LAST WORD
Category: Modern Classic (Shaken)
Base Spirit: Gin
ABV Level: High (25%+)
Complexity: Easy
Glass: Coupe
Ice: None
Method: Shake
Time to Make: 2 minutes
Standard Spec - "The Detroit Equal Parts":
0.75 oz Gin
0.75 oz Green Chartreuse
0.75 oz Maraschino Liqueur
0.75 oz Fresh Lime Juice
Step-by-Step Instructions:
Combine all 4 ingredients
Shake with ice
Strain
Brandied Cherry garnish
Why It Works:
Green Chartreuse is intensely herbal (130 herbs). The lime and maraschino tame it perfectly.
History & Context:
A Prohibition-era drink from the Detroit Athletic Club (1915). It was forgotten for 50 years until Murray Stenson found it in an old book in 2004 in Seattle, sparking a worldwide revival.
